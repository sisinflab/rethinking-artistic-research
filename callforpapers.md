We invite contributions on intelligent visual and interactive interfaces supporting artistic research in the age of generative AI, with a focus on **agency**, **Human-Centred AI**, critical/ethical stances toward AI, and learning ecosystems across academies and universities.

## Topics of interest
- AI based Art and Creativity  
- Human-Centred Interaction Paradigms and Tools for Art and Creativity  
- Learning Ecosystems and Computer-Supported Cooperative Work  
- Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion and Responsible Design  
- Cultural Heritage and Digital Humanities  
- Health, Well-being, and Self-Actualization  

## Submission types
We welcome:

1) **Short papers** (design cases, conceptual frameworks, methodological reflections)  
2) **Demos / prototypes** (including early-stage and speculative interfaces)  
3) **Posters** (work-in-progress, critical reflections, datasets/repositories/platforms)

**Suggested length (can be adjusted):**
- Short papers: **3–6 pages + references**  
- Demo/Poster extended abstracts: **1–3 pages + references**

## Review process
Submissions will undergo peer review based on:
- **Relevance** to the workshop themes  
- **Originality** of contribution  
- **Clarity** of writing and argumentation  

## Proceedings (planned)
Accepted peer-reviewed contributions will be published in an open-access **CEUR-WS.org** workshop proceedings volume. Demo and poster contributions may be included as extended abstracts in the same volume and/or made available via the workshop website and the P+ARTS online repository.
